Caddie Companion — Project Brief
The product. A patent-pending 6-in-1 folding golf multitool. Pocket-sized anodized aluminum body (blue, black, green, red) with mirror-polished stainless tools that fold out Swiss-Army-style. The six functions: divot repair fork, replaceable brass wire brush (cleans club grooves), two magnetic ball markers, T25 Torx driver (for adjustable clubs), knife, and bottle opener. $29, sold direct. Invented and sold solo by the owner (Gabrial); also listed on Etsy and Amazon.
Who it's for. Golfers of all levels who are tired of carrying separate accessories that rattle around the bag — plus gift buyers (spouses, friends, corporate). It's not a stroke-saver; it's a useful, well-made consolidation tool that gets used every round. Honest framing: it's a premium useful novelty, and some golfers' first reaction is "do I need this?" — overcoming that skepticism is a core job of the site.
The site. A single-page site that is simultaneously the homepage and the product page (one product, so no catalog). Built on Shopify. Eight sections: hero → one-line promise → "six tools → one" → the six functions clearly → lifestyle on the course → engineered/specs → short origin story → footer. A persistent "Order Now" button floats throughout; the buy module carries the four color variants.
The design goal. Premium, clean, minimal, "engineered" — Apple/Tesla as touchstones, not the cluttered/salesy golf-store look. The owner's one hard rule: "don't want it to feel like just an online store." So it's magazine/editorial — photo-led, sparse copy, lets the product breathe. White/light background, blue logo accent. Tone target is "premium and fun" — premium is handled, the fun needs to come through in copy voice so it doesn't read sterile.
The visual system. Two photographic worlds, unified by one color grade:

Grass / course world — warm-hearted, real, golf-context. Used for hero, lifestyle, origin. This is the dominant voice ("course companion," not "precision instrument").
Clean studio world — neutral light surface, cool-engineered. Used for the "six vs one" argument, the function breakdown, and the engineered/spec beat.
Everything is graded to one cool-neutral white point so the two worlds read as one brand. The product metal stays neutral-cool always; only natural accents (grass, brass bristles) stay warm. No warm/golden tint — tested and rejected as off-brand.
